## 🛤 Critical Path
1. **Cognitive Load** *(root_cause)*
   When working memory is overloaded — holding too many concerns at once — the mind's ability to think clearly, generate ideas, and process situations is blocked, undermining everything downstream.
2. **Cognitive Process** *(mechanism)*
   When cognitive load is manageable, the mental loop of processing thoughts, stress scenarios, and ideas can function properly, enabling both well-being and strategic self-awareness.
3. **Scenario Awareness** *(moderator)*
   Clear thinking produces awareness of the realistic range of outcomes — worst case, optimal, and ideal — which calibrates expectations and sharpens decision-making.
4. **Decision Quality** *(moderator)*
   Understanding the full landscape of possible outcomes makes career and life decisions more risk-aware and better aligned with personal goals.
5. **Job Change or Startup Decision** *(mechanism)*
   A well-informed decision to change jobs or start a venture is the pivotal action point — influenced by work satisfaction, scenario awareness, and available assets — and opens the path to passive income.
6. **Work Satisfaction** *(mechanism)*
   How fulfilled a person feels at work directly amplifies happiness and, when low, triggers the desire to seek change — making it both a driver of well-being and a catalyst for career decisions.
7. **Happiness** *(effect)*
   Happiness is the culmination of supportive thinking, satisfying work, good health, and expectations that match lived reality — all reinforcing each other over time.

- **Job Change or Startup Decision**
  The central life decision of whether to stay in current employment, switch jobs, or launch a venture. It matters because it determines access to passive income, work satisfaction, and long-term happiness — and the quality of this decision depends heavily on how informed and scenario-aware the person is.

  - Launch a startup or venture → Highest potential for passive income and autonomy, but also highest risk — scenario awareness and asset inventory are critical to success.
